The problem

It should not be all or nothing

Veterans often choose to disconnect completely, both physically and digitally, from other people, from the news and the outside world. We see it every day at the Veterans Hub in Hobart.

Digitally, the experience on phones is either all or nothing. Platform providers create an always-on experience with unfiltered access to a flood of information that is hard to control. For veterans managing hypervigilance and trauma, that is not uncomfortable. It is harmful.

The phone layout we are building is aimed at creating a controlled digital environment, like a controlled physical environment. Just the minimum needed to connect with only the people that matter and only the services you want and need.

Architecture

Four layers. One designed experience.

Not separate tools requiring separate decisions. One environment, built around the person and their actual needs.

Layer 01
UX
A calm, clear, uncluttered home screen. Large icons. High contrast. Nothing designed to provoke anxiety or reward compulsive checking.
Layer 02
Communication
Always on. Phone, messages, and chosen contacts are never restricted. Connection to people is the foundation. Only the flood is managed.
Layer 03
Content taps
Configurable subscriptions the user sets once. DVA updates. Weather. AFL scores. Veterans Hub community. Nothing they didn't choose. No algorithm.
Layer 04
Trusted sources
A verification layer anchored by DVA as Digital Mainstreet. Check a provider before acting. Real-time scam alerts. DVA-endorsed referrals first.
The partnership

Four partners. One proof of concept.

This project is built around four partners. The veteran comes first. Everything else follows from that.

01
The Veteran
The customer is the first and most critical partner. Every design decision, every layer, every setting starts with one question: does this work for the person holding the phone? Veterans at the Veterans Hub in Hobart are the co-designers, the testers, and the measure of whether this project succeeds.
02
Veterans Hub and DVA
Community access and institutional anchor. The Veterans Hub provides the cohort and the trust. DVA provides the Trusted Sources layer its authority, connecting veterans to verified providers and real-time scam alerts through an institution they already know.
03
The Everyday Technologist
Problem definition. Community cohort. Published evidence framework. A public communication platform to translate findings for general audiences.
04
University of Tasmania, SET
Research infrastructure. Academic rigour. Developer capacity. Publication pathway. HDR student opportunities across HCI, digital health and cybersecurity.
05
Industry Partner
Technical infrastructure. Platform access. Commercial pathway. The co-investment that unlocks ARC Linkage Grant funding for the full research program.
